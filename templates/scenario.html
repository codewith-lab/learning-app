{% if category == 'stressed-fearful' %}

Stressed / Fearful Body Language

  • Eyes: Wide pupils
  • Ears: Rotated to the sides (in an "airplane ears" pose) or angled backward
  • Tail: Hold low to the body, tuck under body, or thrash and thump
  • Body: Crouch low to ground, grooming more than usual, hide under furniture or in corners
  • Face: Excessive lip-clicking / yawning, whiskers pulled back
⚠

Watch Out Signs of Severe Level

  • Eyes: Staring
  • Ears: Pinned flat against head
  • Tail: Puffed up like a bottlebrush (to look bigger), flick or thrash
  • Body: Frozen crouch, arched back (to look bigger)
  • Face: Hissing / growling, teeth bared
Common Scenarios:
New to Home (First Few Days)
Loud Noises (Vacuum, Thunder etc.)
Vet Check
Conflict with Another Cat
⚠ Help Me Out!
  • Provide a soundproof hiding spot, give space
  • Keep familiar bedding / toys in new spaces
  • Offer high-value treats – Toss near them
  • [Severe Level] Use feliway diffusers or calming sprays
  • [Severe Level] Isolate immediately - quiet, dark room with litter / food / water
Don't Do This!
  • Stare directly
  • Punishment
  • Force interaction - touch, cuddles, pull out of hiding
{% elif category == 'painful-sick' %}

Painful / Sick Body Language

  • Eyes: Squinting or half-closed, uneven pupil sizes
  • Ears: Flattened or rotated outward, less responsive to sounds
  • Tail: Hold stiffly, low to ground
  • Body: Stiff and hunched posture, legs tucked, reluctant to move, decreased appetite
  • Face: whiskers pulled back, running nose
⚠

Watch Out Signs of Severe Level

  • Eyes: Fully dilated pupils
  • Ears: Hot to touch
  • Tail: Completely limp or rigid
  • Body: Completely collapse, cannot stand, no eating / drinking for 12+ hours
  • Face: Open-mouth panting

If you notice these severe symptoms, please take your cat to the vet immediately!

{% elif category == 'curious-playful' %}

Curious / Playful Body Language

  • Eyes: Big and bright, might be slightly dilated
  • Ears: Up and forward facing
  • Tail: Upright with curled tip ("question mark" shape), or quickly flick their tail from side to side in hunting and watching whatever is captivating their attention
  • Body: Slow stalking movements, crouched with butt wiggles, short bursts of running, occasional paw bats at objects, slight arched back
  • Sound: Soft chirps or trills
⚠

Watch Out the Subtle Difference

  • A quick flicking tail when I am not stalking or paying attention -> annoyed or irritated
  • An arched back and puffed up -> stressed or feared
  • A completely flatten ear -> switching to aggression

We are predators with a natural instinct to hunt. When I want to play, I may act like I am hunting.

{% elif category == 'happy-relaxed' %}

Happy / Relaxed Body Language

  • Eyes: Round or partially closed, pupils narrow like slits, blinks slowly at you ("cat kisses" of trust)
  • Ears: Upright and forward facing
  • Tail: Pointed straight up, may sway gently like a metronome
  • Body: Rolling belly-up, stretching out fully, kneading ("making biscuits" with paws)
  • Face: Cheek rubbing
  • Sound: Gentle purring, short and melodic meows

10 Signs I Trust and Like You

  • Slow Blinking
  • Headbutting
  • Tail Wrapping
  • Kneading
  • Showing Belly
  • Purring
  • Grooming / Licking
  • Greeting You at the Door
  • Following You Everywhere
  • Sleeping Next To You

When I knead you, it can be a sign of affection. Kneading is a behavior that I performed as a kitten with my mother.

Purring has mostly been associated with contentment, excitement, or other positive emotional states. However, I can also purr in stressful situations. It's important to watch my body language as well as looking at the context.
